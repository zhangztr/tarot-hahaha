# Tarot Beginner's Guide

Welcome to Tarot Web! Whether you're completely new to tarot or looking for a refresher, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know.

## What Is Tarot?

Tarot is a deck of 78 cards, each carrying symbolic imagery and meaning. The deck is divided into two main groups:

- **Major Arcana** (22 cards): These represent significant life events, spiritual lessons, and archetypal forces. Cards like *The Fool*, *The Lovers*, and *Death* deal with the big themes of the human experience.
- **Minor Arcana** (56 cards): These cover everyday situations and are divided into four suits — Cups (emotions), Wands (action), Swords (intellect), and Pentacles (material world).

## How to Use This Site

1. **Think of a question** — It can be about love, career, personal growth, or simply "What should I focus on today?" You can also leave it blank and let the cards offer general guidance.
2. **Choose a spread** — We offer three options:
   - *Single Card*: Quick insight into the present moment.
   - *Past · Present · Future*: See how past events shape the present and what lies ahead.
   - *Problem · Obstacle · Advice*: Identify the core issue and find a path forward.
3. **Draw your cards** — Click the button and watch the shuffle animation. Your cards will be revealed one by one.
4. **Read the interpretation** — Each card comes with its meaning in the context of its position in the spread.

## A Few Tips

- You don't need to believe in anything mystical to use tarot. Many people treat it as a tool for self-reflection — the cards help you think about your situation from a different angle.
- There are no "bad" cards. Even cards like *Death* or *The Tower* represent transformation and necessary change, not literal doom.
- Reversed cards (upside-down) often point to blocked energy, internal challenges, or an alternative side of the card's meaning.
